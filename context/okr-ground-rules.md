# OKR Ground Rules — Nexora

Scope: Company-wide
Last updated: 2026-02-21

---

## 1) Structure

- Allowed levels: Company (mandatory), Department (mandatory), Team (optional), Individual (not used)
- Cycle length: Annual objectives with quarterly KR milestones
- Check-in rhythm: Quarterly review (owners), aligned to board cadence
- Focus limits:
  - Company: max 4 Objectives (exception requires CEO sign-off)
  - Department: max 3 Objectives
  - Team: max 2 Objectives
  - KRs per Objective: 2–4 at all levels
  - Exception rule: >3 Objectives at department level requires explicit justification and approval from department head + CEO

## 2) OKR Types

- Aspirational: stretch goals where 60–80% achievement is success. Used for market position, AI differentiation, NRR growth. Score below 60% triggers a retrospective — not punishment.
- Committed: delivery-critical goals expected at 90–100%. Used for compliance (ISO 27001), profitability milestones, critical hires. Miss requires a formal root-cause note.
- Learning: hypothesis goals scored by decision quality, not a number. Scored as: hypothesis validated / invalidated + decision taken. Used for new motions (partner channel, lower-tier churn intervention).

## 3) KR Design Rules

- Input/output/outcome policy: Outcome KRs are the default and preferred. Output KRs allowed when directly and verifiably the strongest predictor of a near-term outcome (e.g. "RBAC shipped by April" when it unlocks €800K ARR). Input KRs not allowed.
- Leading/lagging balance: each Objective should have at least one leading indicator KR
- Baseline/target/timeframe required: yes — every KR must include a baseline, target, and deadline

## 4) Ownership and Alignment

- Objective owner rule: one named owner, accountable for outcome and reporting
- KR owner rule: one named owner. Cross-functional KRs may have a co-owner with decision rights explicitly defined at OKR creation
- Vertical alignment rule: every department Objective must link to a company Objective. Every team Objective must link to a company or department Objective.
- Horizontal alignment rule: cross-team dependencies mapped at cycle start in Oboard. Conflicts resolved within 1 week via planning sync; unresolved escalated to C-level.
- Cascading vs laddering mix: 70% cascade (company → department → team), 30% ladder (team OKR connects up to company Objective, bypassing department)
- Alignment model: Hybrid — cascade for core functions (Sales, CS, Engineering); ladder allowed for cross-functional and innovation-oriented teams
- Misalignment escalation rule: department head resolves within 1 week; escalates to CEO if cross-department

## 5) Incentives

- Bonus/performance linkage policy: no direct linkage to OKR scores
- If linked, linkage method: OKR quality, ambition, and learning behaviors (not raw score attainment) are discussed in annual performance conversations
- Anti-gaming safeguards: stretch goal scoring 60–70% is explicitly framed as success. Sandbagging (setting low targets to ensure 100%) flagged during OKR review as a quality issue, not a performance win.

## 6) Transparency and Tracking

- Source of truth tool/document: Oboard
- Visibility rule: fully open to all Nexora employees (Transparent by Default)
- Update cadence and owner: KR status refreshed weekly by owners
- Review/reset ritual: quarterly review — each department presents progress, blockers, and forecast to leadership before board check-in. Annual reset each January. Mid-year amendments allowed with department head + CEO approval.
