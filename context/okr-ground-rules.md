# OKR Ground Rules — Nexora

**Scope:** Company-wide
**Created:** February 25, 2026

---

## 1) Structure

- **Levels:** Company only (team-level OKRs introduced in future cycles)
- **Cycle length:** Annual (2026)
- **Check-in rhythm:** Quarterly — reviewed by full leadership team
- **Focus limits:** Max 4 Objectives, 2–4 KRs per Objective

## 2) OKR Types

- **Aspirational:** Stretch goals; 70% achievement = success (used for AI moat, customer growth bets)
- **Committed:** Delivery-critical; ~100% expected (used for profitability path, enterprise compliance milestones)
- **Learning:** Hypothesis goals scored by decision quality — what was validated/invalidated and what changed (used for new bets with high uncertainty)
- Each Objective must be labeled with its type at the start of the cycle

## 3) KR Design

- Outcome KRs are the default — measure the change, not the activity
- Output KRs allowed when they are the strongest near-term predictor of an outcome
- Input KRs not allowed
- Every KR must have: baseline value, target value, and deadline
- Balance leading indicators (predictive) and lagging indicators (results)

## 4) Ownership and Alignment

- **Ownership model:** One named C-suite owner per Objective; one named department lead per KR; contributors listed separately
- **Vertical alignment rule:** Each Objective maps explicitly to one of the CEO's 4 strategic bets (Profitability / Enterprise / AI Moat / Customer Growth)
- **Horizontal alignment rule:** Cross-functional dependencies surfaced and resolved at quarterly check-ins; leadership team is the arbitration layer
- **Alignment model:** Classic top-down for this first company-wide cycle; revisit as team-level OKRs are introduced

## 5) Incentives

- No direct linkage to bonuses or performance reviews
- OKR quality, ambition, and learning behavior may be considered informally in leadership performance conversations
- **Safeguard:** Sandbagging flagged at quarterly review if targets appear set to guarantee 100% on aspirational goals

## 6) Transparency and Tracking

- **Source of truth:** Google Drive (Nexora — Company Docs folder)
- **Visibility rule:** Fully open to all 280 employees (aligns with "Transparent by Default" company value)
- **Update cadence:** KR status refreshed by each owner before every quarterly check-in
