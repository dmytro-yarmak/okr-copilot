# Nexora All-Hands Meeting Notes

**Date:** January 24, 2026
**Location:** Amsterdam HQ (streamed to London & NYC)
**Attendees:** ~240 (of 280 total)
**Notes taken by:** Sophie Bakker, Chief of Staff

---

## Opening — Maria van den Berg, CEO

Maria opened with a thank-you to the team for a "breakout year." Hit the gong for crossing €24M ARR (tradition when we hit a milestone).

Quick highlights she shared:
- We grew from 180 to 280 people this year — "look around, almost half the people in this room joined in the last 12 months"
- 340 customers now trust us with their L&D
- Enterprise tier launched and already at 28 customers
- We've expanded to 3 offices across 2 continents

"But I'm not going to sugarcoat things either." Maria flagged a few areas of concern:
- Our churn rate, especially in smaller accounts, is "not where it needs to be"
- Sales cycles are getting longer — "buyers are nervous and we need to be patient but also creative"
- We've grown fast and there are "growing pains" — processes that worked at 100 people don't work at 280

Maria said 2026 theme is **"Sustainable Excellence"** — balancing growth with efficiency and quality.

---

## CTO Update — Thomas Richter

Thomas gave a product + engineering update:

**Product highlights:**
- AI skill-gap analyzer V2 is live — early data shows 32% of customer base actively using
- Workday integration shipped, Slack integration in beta
- Enterprise features (SOC2, SSO, custom reporting) all delivered on schedule

**Engineering reality check:**
- "We need to be honest — our velocity has slowed down." Sprint velocity down ~15% in Q4.
- Root cause: technical debt in the content delivery module and learning path engine
- Thomas announced a "Tech Health" initiative — dedicating 20% of engineering capacity to debt reduction in Q1
- Hiring: 12 open engineering roles, including 4 ML engineers. "If you know great engineers, please refer them. Especially ML folks — that market is brutal."

**Upcoming priorities for H1:**
- AI-generated learning paths (alpha by end of Q1)
- Predictive skill readiness dashboard
- Performance improvements — "page load times have crept up and customers notice"
- Security hardening for enterprise (ISO 27001 prep)

---

## CRO Update — James Whitfield

James walked through revenue and go-to-market:

**Numbers:**
- Q4 was strong: €2.4M in new ARR
- Pipeline for Q1 2026 is healthy: €8.5M in qualified pipeline
- BUT conversion rates are down — win rate dropped from 28% to 23% in Q4
- Average deal cycle: 62 days (was 45 days a year ago)

"LearnHub is getting more aggressive. They raised $80M and they're starting to show up in our deals. We used to win on 'they're too expensive and too complex.' Now they're launching a 'LearnHub Lite' that targets mid-market."

**James' priorities:**
- Improve sales enablement — "our AEs need better competitive battle cards"
- Focus on enterprise expansion — ACV in enterprise is 3x our standard tier
- Partner channel — "we barely have a partner program and that's leaving revenue on the table"

New hire spotlight: Lisa Chen joined as VP Enterprise Sales from Salesforce.

---

## CCO Update — Priya Sharma

Priya covered customer success:

- **NPS:** 42 (up from 38 a year ago, industry avg is ~35)
- **Net Revenue Retention:** 110%
- **Annual churn:** 12% blended. Priya broke this down:
  - Enterprise (1000+ employees): 4% churn
  - Mid-tier (500-1000): 8% churn
  - Lower tier (<500): 18% churn — "this is where we're bleeding"

"The lower tier customers often don't have a dedicated L&D person. They buy our platform with great intentions but don't have the bandwidth to drive adoption. We need to figure out if we should invest in helping them succeed or focus our energy upmarket."

**Priorities for Priya's team:**
- Launch automated onboarding playbook for lower-tier customers
- Build customer health scoring model
- Target: get NRR to 120%+ through expansion motions
- "I want to launch a Nexora Customer Advisory Board — our best customers have amazing ideas"

**Support metrics:**
- Ticket volume up 35% QoQ — added 3 support engineers in Q4
- Average first response time: 4.2 hours (target: <2 hours)
- Customer satisfaction on support interactions: 4.1/5

## CPO (People) Update — Anna Visser

Anna covered people and culture:

- 280 employees now. 55% hired in last 18 months.
- Attrition: 14% annualized — "too high. Exit interviews point to career growth concerns and compensation competitiveness."
- Key focus: building manager capability. "Most of our managers were promoted from IC roles in the last year. They need support."
- Launching a manager development program in Q1 (using our own platform, naturally!)
- Employee engagement survey coming in February

DEI note: leadership team is 40% women, engineering is 22% women. "We're making progress but not fast enough."

Hiring plan for 2026: ~45 new hires planned (net), focused on Engineering, Sales, and Customer Success.

- Amsterdam: 160 employees
- London: 65 employees
- New York: 55 employees
