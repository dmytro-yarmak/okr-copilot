# Q4 2025 Board Update — Nexora

**Prepared by:** Maria van den Berg, CEO
**Date:** January 12, 2026
**Classification:** Board Confidential

---

## Executive Summary

Q4 was a strong finish to a transformational year. We crossed the €24M ARR milestone, up from €16M at end of 2024 — representing 50% YoY growth. Our enterprise tier, launched in early 2025, is showing strong initial traction and is reshaping our deal pipeline toward higher-value contracts.

However, growth came at a cost. Burn rate remains elevated and we need to demonstrate a credible path to profitability within 2026 to position for a potential Series C or bridge if needed.

---

## Financial Highlights

### Revenue

- **ARR:** €24M (end of Q4), up from €16M (end of 2024)
- **Q4 new ARR added:** €2.4M
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR):** 110%
- **Average Contract Value:** €70.6K (up from €53K in 2024)

### Margins & Efficiency

- **Gross margin:** 68% (down slightly from 70% in Q3 due to professional services costs for enterprise onboarding)
- **CAC payback period:** 18 months (target: reduce to <15 months in 2026)
- **Burn multiple:** 1.8x (improved from 2.3x in Q4 2024)

### Cash Position

- **Cash on hand:** €18.2M
- **Monthly burn:** ~€850K
- **Runway:** ~21 months at current burn

---

## Customer Metrics

- **Total customers:** 340 (up from 230 at end of 2024)
- **Enterprise tier customers (1000+ employees):** 28
- **Logo churn:** 12% annually (concentrated in lower tiers — our <500 employee segment churns at 18%, while 500+ churns at only 6%)
- **Platform NPS:** 42 (industry average: 35)

---

## Headcount

- **Total employees:** 280 (up from 180 at end of 2024)
- **Key hires in Q4:** VP of Enterprise Sales, Head of AI, 3 senior ML engineers
- **Attrition rate:** 14% annualized (above our target of 10%)
- **Open roles:** 22 (heavily weighted toward Engineering and Sales)

---

## Product Updates

- Enterprise tier now includes SOC2 Type II compliance, SSO, and custom reporting
- AI skill-gap analyzer V2 launched — early adoption metrics promising (32% of customers actively using)
- New integration with Workday went live in November
- Technical debt becoming a concern — sprint velocity declined ~15% in Q4 as engineers dealt with legacy architecture in the content delivery module

---

## Fundraising & Investor Notes

Series B (€35M raised March 2024) provides adequate runway through 2027 at reduced burn. Board consensus is that demonstrating path to cash-flow positive is more important than raising a Series C in current market conditions.

Key investor question from last board meeting: "Can Nexora achieve 50% growth again in 2026 while also improving unit economics?" Maria's view: Yes, but it requires disciplined execution on enterprise expansion and churn reduction.

---

## Risks & Concerns

1. **Sales cycle elongation** — average deal cycle extended from 45 to 62 days in Q4. Economic uncertainty is making procurement teams more cautious.
2. **AI talent market** — we lost two ML engineers to a large tech company. Compensation benchmarking needed.
3. **LearnHub (main competitor)** raised $80M Series D in November. They're investing heavily in AI and beginning to move downmarket toward our segment.
4. **Customer support** ticket volume up 35% QoQ — growing pains from rapid customer acquisition.

---

## Looking Ahead to 2026

Maria outlined the following priorities for the leadership team offsite (scheduled February 2026):

1. Define the path to cash-flow positive — what does the cost structure need to look like?
2. Double down on enterprise — this is where our best unit economics are
3. Widen the AI moat — our skill-gap analyzer is what wins deals, we need to stay ahead
4. Fix the churn problem — especially in the lower tier, we're leaking revenue
