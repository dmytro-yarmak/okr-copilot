# Competitive Landscape Analysis — Q1 2026

**Prepared by:** Marketing Intelligence Team
**Date:** January 2026
**Owner:** James Whitfield, CRO

---

## Market Overview

The L&D Technology market is estimated at €12B globally and growing at ~15% CAGR. The mid-market segment (companies with 200-2000 employees) represents approximately €3.2B of this market and is the fastest-growing segment at ~20% CAGR.

**Key market dynamics:**
- Shift from compliance-driven learning to skills-based talent development
- AI disrupting both content creation and learning personalization
- Growing demand for measurable learning ROI
- Economic pressure forcing L&D teams to do more with less
- Consolidation: 3 acquisitions in the space in 2025

## Competitor Profiles

### LearnHub — Primary Competitor

- **HQ:** San Francisco
- **Founded:** 2015
- **Funding:** $180M total (Series D closed Nov 2025 — $80M)
- **Estimated ARR:** $85M
- **Customers:** ~1,200 (primarily enterprise, 2000+ employees)
- **Employees:** ~600

**Strengths:**
- Strong brand recognition in enterprise
- Deep integration ecosystem (200+ integrations)
- Recently launched "LearnHub Lite" targeting mid-market — DIRECT competitive threat
- Significant AI investment post-Series D
- Large customer success team

**Weaknesses:**
- Complex implementation (3-6 months typical)
- UI feels dated compared to Nexora
- Pricing is high — typical mid-market deal is 40% more than Nexora
- AI capabilities still behind ours (their skill mapping is rules-based, not ML)
- "LearnHub Lite" is a stripped-down version, not purpose-built for mid-market

**Recent moves:**
- Hired VP of Mid-Market Sales in October 2025
- Announced "LearnHub AI" features at their conference (mostly vaporware so far)
- Showing up in our competitive deals more frequently — win rate against LearnHub dropped from 65% to 52% in Q4

**THREAT LEVEL: HIGH and increasing**

### SkillForge — Secondary Competitor

- **HQ:** London
- **Founded:** 2017
- **Funding:** €22M (Series A)
- **Estimated ARR:** €8M
- **Customers:** ~500 (primarily SMB, <200 employees)
- **Employees:** ~120

**Strengths:**
- Very easy to set up (self-serve, no implementation needed)
- Strong in SMB — good product-led growth motion
- Affordable pricing
- Growing fast in UK market

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited enterprise features (no SSO, basic reporting)
- No AI capabilities worth mentioning
- Weak in North America
- Small team limits product development speed

**Recent moves:**
- Trying to move upmarket but struggling with feature gaps
- Launched a "Teams" tier for 200-500 employee companies
- Rumored to be exploring acquisition targets

**THREAT LEVEL: LOW (but watch for upmarket move)**

### Legacy LMS Players

**Cornerstone OnDemand**
- Massive installed base in enterprise
- Complex, expensive, hard to implement
- Customers increasingly frustrated — we win 70% of competitive deals against Cornerstone
- Their "modernization" efforts have been slow

**SAP SuccessFactors Learning**
- Bundled with SAP HCM — captive audience
- Terrible standalone experience
- We struggle to displace when customer is deep in SAP ecosystem
- Not really a competitive threat outside SAP shops

**Docebo**
- Publicly traded, strong in enterprise
- Better UX than Cornerstone/SAP but not as modern as Nexora
- Growing AI capabilities — worth watching
- Occasionally compete in upper mid-market deals

**THREAT LEVEL: MEDIUM (Docebo), LOW (others — dying slowly)**

## Win/Loss Analysis — Q4 2025

**Total competitive deals analyzed:** 87
**Overall win rate:** 23% (down from 28% in Q3)

| Competitor | Win Rate | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| vs LearnHub | 52% | Down from 65% |
| vs SkillForge | 78% | Stable |
| vs Cornerstone | 70% | Stable |
| vs Docebo | 55% | Slight decline |
| vs SAP | 45% | Low sample, mostly SAP-committed orgs |

**Top reasons we WIN:**
1. AI skill-gap analyzer (cited in 73% of wins) — "nothing else comes close"
2. Speed of implementation (cited in 61% of wins)
3. User experience / modern design (cited in 58% of wins)
4. Customer references and NPS (cited in 42% of wins)

**Top reasons we LOSE:**
1. Price (cited in 38% of losses) — not that we're expensive, but buyers under budget pressure
2. Enterprise feature gaps (cited in 31% of losses) — especially security, compliance, RBAC
3. Integration breadth (cited in 24% of losses) — LearnHub has 200+ integrations, we have 45
4. Brand recognition (cited in 19% of losses) — "we've never heard of Nexora"

## Competitive Positioning

**Our sweet spot:**
- Mid-market companies (200-2000 employees)
- With a dedicated L&D function (even if small)
- That value modern UX and AI capabilities
- And need fast time-to-value (weeks, not months)

**Where we struggle:**
- Very large enterprises (2000+) — feature gaps in security/compliance
- Very small companies (<200) — they can't justify our pricing
- SAP-heavy organizations — ecosystem lock-in
- Budget-constrained buyers in the current macro environment

---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Close the enterprise gap fast** — ISO 27001, advanced RBAC, and SCIM provisioning are table stakes. Every quarter we don't have these, we lose deals.
2. **Build the integration moat** — We have 45 integrations. LearnHub has 200+. We don't need 200, but we need the top 30 that matter.
3. **Invest in brand** — We're still unknown outside our customer base. Need thought leadership, analyst relations (Gartner, Forrester), and G2 #1 ranking.
4. **Arm the sales team** — Updated competitive battle cards needed ASAP, especially for LearnHub Lite positioning.
5. **Protect the AI advantage** — Our AI skill-gap analyzer is the single biggest competitive differentiator. The AI roadmap is critical.
