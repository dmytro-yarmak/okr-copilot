# Strategy Memo 2026 — From the CEO

**TO:** Nexora Leadership Team
**FROM:** Maria van den Berg, CEO
**DATE:** February 3, 2026
**RE:** Our Strategic Direction for 2026

---

Team,

I want to share my thinking on where we're headed this year. After three years of "growth mode," 2026 is the year we prove that Nexora is a durable, sustainable business — not just a fast-growing one.

I'll be candid: the easy growth is behind us. The macro environment is tighter, buyers are more cautious, and our biggest competitor just raised $80M. We can't outspend them. We have to outsmart them.

## Where We've Been

When we started Nexora in 2019, the L&D tech market was dominated by legacy LMS platforms that were built for compliance, not learning. We saw the gap: mid-market companies needed modern, intelligent learning tools — not dumbed-down enterprise platforms.

We've come a long way. 340 customers. €24M ARR. A product that people genuinely love (NPS 42 in a space where 30 is considered good). A team of 280 talented people across three offices.

## Our Vision Remains Unchanged

> *"Every employee empowered to grow into their full potential."*

This is why we exist. Learning should be personalized, continuous, and directly connected to career growth — not a compliance checkbox.

## What I Believe About 2026

Here are the four strategic bets I want us to make:

### 1. Path to Profitability

We need to get to cash-flow positive by end of Q4 2026. Not because we're running out of money (we have 21 months of runway), but because this is about building the right muscle. We've been a "spend to grow" company. Now we need to become a "grow efficiently" company.

This means:
- Improving gross margins (target: 75%, currently 68%)
- Being smarter about where we invest in acquisition
- Automating what we can in onboarding and support

This does NOT mean: cutting investment in product or engineering. Our technology is what wins.

### 2. Win the Enterprise Mid-Market

Our enterprise tier is only 7 months old and already has 28 customers. The unit economics are dramatically better: lower churn, higher ACV, better NRR. I want us to become the unquestioned #1 platform for companies with 200-2000 employees.

This means investing in:
- Security and compliance (SOC2 is done, but we need GDPR tooling, ISO 27001)
- Customization and configuration capabilities
- A sales motion built for larger deal sizes

### 3. AI as Our Moat

Our AI skill-gap analyzer is why customers choose us over the competition. In every win/loss analysis, it's the #1 cited reason. But we're sitting on a goldmine of data and only scratching the surface.

I want us to launch bold new AI capabilities this year:
- AI-generated learning paths (not just recommendations, full curriculum design)
- Predictive analytics for skill readiness
- AI coaching assistant for managers

If we do this right, we don't just have a feature advantage — we have a data advantage that compounds over time.

### 4. Make Customers Our Growth Engine

Our churn rate at 12% is too high. Every customer we lose is a customer we need to re-acquire. But beyond just reducing churn, I want our existing customers to become our primary growth engine through:
- Expansion revenue (teams buying more seats, upgrading tiers)
- Referrals and case studies (our best marketing is happy customers)
- Community building (Nexora user groups, annual conference?)

## Our Values Guide How We Execute

A reminder of what we stand for:
- **Learn Relentlessly** — we practice what we preach
- **Customer Obsessed** — their success is our success
- **Ship & Iterate** — done is better than perfect
- **Transparent by Default** — open communication builds trust
- **Win as One Team** — no silos

I know this is ambitious. But I've never been more confident in this team's ability to deliver. Let's make 2026 the year Nexora became unstoppable.

See you at the leadership offsite on Feb 20-21.

Maria
