## 2026 Strategic Priorities (CEO — Maria van den Berg)

### 1. Path to Profitability
Get to cash-flow positive by end of Q4 2026. Improve gross margins to 75% (from 68%). Automate onboarding and support. Reduce cloud infrastructure costs. Be smarter about acquisition spend.

### 2. Win the Enterprise Mid-Market
Become the unquestioned #1 platform for companies with 200–2000 employees. Enterprise tier (launched mid-2025) already has 28 customers with dramatically better unit economics: lower churn, higher ACV, better NRR. Invest in security/compliance, configurability, and an enterprise-scale sales motion.

### 3. AI as Our Moat
The AI skill-gap analyzer is the #1 cited win reason (73% of competitive wins). Expand AI capabilities: AI-generated learning paths, predictive skill readiness, AI manager coaching. Build a data advantage that compounds over time.

### 4. Make Customers Our Growth Engine
Every churned customer must be re-acquired. Push NRR from 110% to 125%+ through expansion revenue, customer referrals, case studies, and community. Fix the lower-tier churn problem.

## Key Tensions & Trade-offs for OKR Design

1. **Growth vs. Efficiency:** 50% ARR growth in 2025 came at the cost of elevated burn and operational strain. 2026 must balance both.

2. **Enterprise focus vs. SMB churn:** Doubling down on enterprise (right unit economics) while lower-tier churn bleeds €910K/quarter. Need clarity on which lower-tier customers are worth saving.

3. **AI investment vs. tech debt:** AI is the competitive moat, but 15% velocity decline from tech debt threatens delivery. 20% dedicated capacity to debt may not be enough.

4. **Hiring urgency vs. quality:** 22 open roles (especially ML engineers) are blocking both the AI roadmap and revenue growth. Compensation competitiveness is a blocker.

5. **Support scaling vs. automation:** Ticket volume up 35% QoQ. Hiring alone won't solve this — automation and self-serve are critical but take time to build.
