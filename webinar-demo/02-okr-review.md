# Demo 02: `$okr-review`

## Purpose

Show how Codex refines a weak draft into a focused, measurable OKR.

## Before Draft (Intentionally Weak)

```text
Objective:
Improve customer experience for Growth accounts.

Key Results:
1) Run onboarding webinars every month.
2) Send more customer emails.
3) Reduce churn a lot.

Owner: Customer Success team
Timeframe: 2026
```

## Copy-Paste Prompt

```text
$okr-review

Yehor wants refinement mode (not strict audit).
Please improve the draft below while keeping the same intent.
Keep it simple for business audience and tie recommendations to Nexora context.

[paste the weak draft]
```

## Expected Output (Abridged)

```text
OKR Review Complete

Objective:
Improve Growth-account customer experience by increasing early adoption and reducing avoidable churn in Q2 2026.

Type: Committed
Level: Team
Timeframe: Apr 1, 2026 -> Jun 30, 2026
Owner: Growth Customer Success Lead

Key Results:
1) Increase 30-day activation in Growth accounts from 12% to 30% by Jun 30, 2026.
   Owner: Onboarding Program Manager
2) Improve 14-day onboarding milestone completion from 41% to 70% by Jun 30, 2026.
   Owner: CS Operations Manager
3) Reduce annualized Growth churn from 18% to 14% by Jun 30, 2026.
   Owner: Growth CSM Team Lead

Quality: Green
Strategy fit: Strong
Confidence: 7/10
Commitment: 8/10

Tracking location: Gainsight dashboard
Check-in rhythm: Weekly
```

## What Improved

- Vague objective became specific and time-bound.
- Activity KRs became measurable outcome/output KRs.
- Baselines and targets were added.
- Ownership changed from generic team ownership to named roles.

## Presenter Talk Track (30-45 sec)

"Review mode keeps the direction but fixes quality gaps. Same strategy, stronger execution design. This is the fastest way to turn fuzzy draft text into an operating OKR."
